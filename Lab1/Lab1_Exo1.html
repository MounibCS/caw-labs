Brief History

The history of Palestine stretches back thousands of years, making it one of the oldest regions inhabited by humans. Ancient civilizations such as the Canaanites and Philistines once thrived in this land, which later became significant in the rise of major religions including Judaism, Christianity, and Islam. Its location at the crossroads of Africa and Asia made it a vital center for trade, culture, and conquest throughout antiquity.

During the Roman and later the Byzantine periods, Palestine saw major transformations, including the spread of Christianity. The region was subsequently conquered by Arab Muslim forces in the 7th century, leading to centuries of Islamic rule. Under the Ottoman Empire, which governed the area from the early 16th century until World War I, Palestine maintained a largely agricultural and multiethnic character.

After the fall of the Ottoman Empire, the land came under British Mandate control, a period that saw increasing tensions between Jewish and Arab communities. In 1948, the establishment of the State of Israel and the subsequent Arab-Israeli conflict dramatically reshaped the region. Since then, the struggle for Palestinian self-determination and sovereignty has continued, defining much of the modern political landscape of the Middle East.
